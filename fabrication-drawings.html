Fabrication packages

Fabrication drawings: because guessing is expensive

Even small “garage builds” benefit from clear drawings. A solid package reduces rework, avoids wasted material, and makes quoting and fabrication predictable.

Capabilities

  • Reverse engineering (from parts, measurements, reference geometry)
  • 3D modelling for fit, function, and manufacturability
  • Custom designs, small and large
  • Part drawings, weldments, assemblies, and BOM support (as needed)

Drawing Types

  • Part drawings (critical dimensions, tolerances, notes)
  • Weldment drawings (weld symbols, cut lists, assemblies)
  • Assembly drawings (views, callouts, fastener notes)
  • Basic GD&T where appropriate

GD&T Weldments Assemblies

Why it matters

  • Reduces shop time and floor-level improvisation
  • Improves repeatability for future builds
  • Makes quoting more accurate and defensible
  • Avoids costly mistakes in material, machining, and welding

Tools & Workflow

  • Fusion 360 modelling + drawing output
  • File exports: PDF, STEP, DXF, DWG (as required)
  • Revision control: clear versioning and “Issued” packages

Security & File Sharing

Project files are shared using reputable cloud storage with access controls. Sensitive files can be shared using time-limited links. Exports can be watermarked on request.

  • Least-privilege access (only what’s needed)
  • Clear handoff packages (no mystery attachments)

Prototyping add-on

For small parts and fit checks, prototyping via 3D printing can shorten iteration time and reduce shop rework.

  • Quick fit-check prints
  • Small jigs, fixtures, brackets (material dependent)

Typical Lead Times

  • Single part / small revision: 2–5 business days
  • Small assemblies: 1–2 weeks
  • Larger packages: 2–6 weeks

Complexity and input quality affect timelines.

Budget Ranges

  • Single part modelling + drawing: $200–$650
  • Weldment drawing package: $450–$1,800
  • Assembly package: $900–$4,500+

Final pricing depends on scope, revision requirements, and deliverable format.

Project Examples

  • Brackets, mounts, jigs
  • Welded frames and supports
  • Small mechanisms and assemblies
  • Repair parts (reverse engineered)
